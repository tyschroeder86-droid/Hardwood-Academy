The Training Experience

What Happens Every Session.

No two sessions are identical — but every single one follows a structure designed to maximize improvement. Here's exactly what your player can expect when they step on the court with Coach Ty.

Session Structure

60 minutes. Zero wasted reps.

Every session is built around your player — not a generic curriculum. Here's how a typical 1-on-1 session flows from start to finish.

0–5min

Warm-Up & Check-In

Dynamic movement, ball-handling warm-ups, and a quick check-in on how your player is feeling. Coach Ty also reviews notes from the last session to pick up exactly where they left off.

5–20min

Fundamentals Focus

Targeted work on the specific fundamental that needs the most attention — ball handling, footwork, shooting form, or defensive stance. High repetition, real-time feedback on every rep.

20–40min

Skill Development Block

The heart of the session. Progressive drills that build on the fundamentals — from stationary to moving, from controlled to game-speed. Drills are chosen specifically for your player's goals.

40–55min

Competitive Application

Skills get tested in competitive situations — 1-on-1 games, shooting challenges, and timed drills. This is where the practice transfers to real game instincts. Players learn to perform under pressure.

55–60min

Cool-Down & Progress Notes

Coach Ty documents what was worked on, what improved, and what to focus on next session. Parents receive a quick verbal recap so everyone stays aligned on the player's development.

What We Train

Drills built for real games.

Every drill Coach Ty uses has a direct connection to game situations. No gimmicks — just the fundamentals that win games at every level.

🏀

Ball Handling & Dribbling

Two-ball drills, cone sequences, pressure dribbling, and change-of-direction moves that build confident, instinctive handles in traffic.

All Levels
🎯

Shooting Mechanics

Form shooting from close range, progressive catch-and-shoot reps, off-the-dribble pull-ups, and free throw consistency — from the ground up.

All Levels
⚡

Footwork & Finishing

Euro steps, hop steps, drop steps, and layup packages from both sides. Footwork is the foundation of everything — we make it automatic.

Intermediate+
👁️

Passing & Court Vision

Chest passes, bounce passes, skip passes, and reading the defense. Players learn when to attack, when to kick, and how to see the whole court.

All Levels
🛡️

Defensive Positioning

Defensive stance, on-ball pressure, closeouts, and help-side positioning. Defense wins games — we build the habits that coaches love.

All Levels
🧠

Game Confidence & Mindset

Mental reps matter. Players learn to handle mistakes, compete under pressure, and build the confidence that translates directly to game performance.

Every Session
For Parents

What to expect first session.

If this is your player's first time training with Coach Ty, here's exactly how it goes.

01

Arrive Ready to Play

Athletic shoes and a water bottle are all you need. Coach Ty brings all balls and equipment. Show up 5 minutes early if it's your first session.

02

Assessment First

The first 10–15 minutes, Coach Ty watches your player move and handles the ball. No pressure — it's just about seeing where they are so training can be built around them.

03

Real Work, Real Fun

The rest of the session dives straight into fundamentals. Players are challenged — but in a way that keeps energy high and confidence building from rep one.

04

Parent Recap

After the session, Coach Ty gives you a quick summary — what stood out, what needs work, and what the plan looks like going forward. No guessing.
